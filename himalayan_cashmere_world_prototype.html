1. Sourcing ethically

The High-Alpine Undercoat

To survive extreme sub-zero temperatures of -40°C in winter, the Himalayan goat grows an extremely fine undercoat of thermal wool. This raw wool is ethically combed by local nomadic herders in the spring, protecting the goats and creating the raw luxurious ingredient for our collection.

The Longevity Method

How to Care for Cashmere

1. Wash Rarely

Cashmere has natural self-cleaning oils that repel dirt and odors. Try hanging your wrap outdoors on a crisp, dry day to refresh it. When necessary, gently hand-wash in cold water with delicate baby soap or natural olive detergent.

2. Dry & Press

Never wring or twist cashmere. Lay it flat on a clean dry towel and gently roll to absorb remaining water. Reshape and air-dry flat away from direct sunlight. Cool iron through a damp cotton pressing cloth to restore pristine loft.

3. Cedar Protection

Store your clean items folded flat inside your organic flax pouch with a natural cedar block. This protects raw fibers from moisture and moths. Never hang premium luxury knitwear, as this can distort its shape over time.
